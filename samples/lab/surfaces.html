Parametrization examples

These examples show parametrically rendered two-dimensional surfaces.

A surface is described parametrically with a function f ( u , v ) = ( x , y , z ) T from parameters (u, v) to a three-dimensional position vector. The domains of u and v vary per parametrization.

In the examples below, parameter n is the number of samples in each of the two directions. The computation complexity is therefore n squared. Parameter m is the density of the normal and other vectors. If m equals 1 then arrows are shown for every line segment. If m equals 2 then are shown every other segment. For m equals 3 every third segment. And so on. Reducing the number of arrows helps performance. Parameter d is a scaling factor for the arrows. Increasing d increases the arrow sizes.

Plane

Parametrization of a plane.

f ( u , v ) = ( x , y , z ) T with

  • x = u
  • y = v
  • z = a + b x + c y
where a is an offset, b , and c are scaling factors, -8 ≤ u ≤ 8 , and -8 ≤ v ≤ 8

Wave

Plot of a surface built from the cosine function.

f ( u , v ) = ( x , y , z ) T with

  • x = u
  • y = v
  • z = a cos ( r c )
where r = u 2 + v 2 , a and c are scaling factors, -10 ≤ u ≤ 10 , and -10 ≤ v ≤ 10

Saddle

Plot of a saddle surface. The surface is rotated around the z-axis.

Note that the product for z make the unit of the z dimensions metre squared.

f ( u , v ) = ( x , y , z ) T with

  • x = u cos (α) - v sin (α)
  • y = u sin (α) + v cos (α)
  • z = a x y
where α is the angle of rotation, a is a scaling factor, -5 ≤ u ≤ 5 , and -5 ≤ v ≤ 5

Ellipsoid

Parametrization of an ellipsoid surface.

f ( u , v ) = ( x , y , z ) T with

  • x = a cos (u) sin (v)
  • y = b sin (u) sin (v)
  • z = c cos (v)
where a , b , and c are scaling factors in the three dimensions, 0 ≤ u ≤ 2π , and 0 ≤ v ≤ π

Torus

Parametrization of a torus surface.

f ( u , v ) = ( x , y , z ) T with

  • x = ( a + b cos ( u )) cos ( v )
  • y = ( a + b cos ( u )) sin ( v )
  • z = b sin ( u )
where a is the major radius, and b the minor radius, 0 ≤ u ≤ 2π , and 0 ≤ v ≤ 2π

Möbius

The Möbius strip.

f ( u , v ) = ( x , y , z ) T with

  • x = a cos ( u ) ( c + v cos ( 1 2 u ))
  • x = a sin ( u ) ( c + v cos ( 1 2 u ))
  • z = a v sin ( 1 2 u )
where a and c are scaling factors, -π ≤ u ≤ π , and -0.5 ≤ v ≤ 0.5

The blue and purple paths are in a diagonal direction, yet they terminate at the starting point.

The orange line along the surface also terminates a its starting point, showing there is just one edge.
